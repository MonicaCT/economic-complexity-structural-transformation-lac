# Claims Audit

This audit reviewed causal and prescriptive language in public narrative files. The paper and README were revised to use observational language where no causal identification exists.

| File/section | Original or risky wording | Problem | Correction | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `paper/main.qmd`, research question | "helps explain divergent structural transformation" | Could be read as causal explanation. | Reframed as "help characterize divergent structural transformation patterns" and descriptive analysis. | Corrected |
| `paper/main.qmd`, econometric evidence | "raising ECI mechanically raises growth" | Explicit causal claim would be unsupported. | Text now states this is not proven and models are observational associations. | Corrected |
| `paper/policy_brief.qmd`, risks | "Policy should balance" | Could sound prescriptive without feasibility evidence. | Rewritten as policy use and screening guidance, with sector-study caveat. | Corrected |
| `README.md`, key findings | Product opportunity categories | Could be read as investment advice. | README now states rankings are screening tools, not prescriptions. | Corrected |
| `dashboard/app.R`, Opportunity Lab | Ranked product table | User may interpret ordering as recommendation. | Visible note added: rankings are screening tools, not investment prescriptions. | Corrected |

Residual risk: terms such as "implication" and "policy" remain where they describe interpretation, not identified causal effects.
