# ECI And PCI Technical Validation

ECI is recomputed year by year from the binary RCA matrix using the country-side normalized reflection matrix `D_c^-1 M D_p^-1 M'`. The second eigenvector is selected, standardized within year and sign-oriented to correlate positively with diversity.

Because ECI is standardized separately each year, it supports within-year comparison and ranking. Changes over time should be described as changes in relative position within the annual ECI distribution, not as absolute units of productive capability gained or lost.

PCI is a projected product index: product values are obtained by projecting country ECI through the product side of the RCA matrix and standardizing within year. It is not a separate external product-side eigen-decomposition.

Validation summary from Phase 2: annual ECI means are effectively zero after standardization; annual ECI standard deviations equal one; annual PCI standard deviations equal one.

Interpretation rule: use language such as "Bolivia's relative position in the annual ECI distribution" rather than "Bolivia gained or lost ECI units of productive capabilities."
