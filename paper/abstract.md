# Abstract

Latin America faces a persistent structural transformation challenge: growth episodes have often coexisted with export baskets concentrated in primary and resource-linked activities. This paper studies economic complexity, productive capabilities and diversification opportunities in Latin America, with Bolivia as a focal case. It uses a locally reconstructed Harvard Atlas HS92 four-digit country-product-year panel covering 6,497,429 observations, 242 countries, 1,243 products and 1995-2023, combined with processed country-year macro controls. The analysis computes RCA, diversity, ubiquity, HHI, entropy, annually standardized ECI, projected PCI, Product Space proximity, density and opportunity scores. Bolivia records ECI = -1.236, diversity = 82, HHI = 0.124 and primary export share = 62.7 percent in 2023. A strict absolute opportunity screen finds few close high-complexity candidates; a relative Bolivia-specific screen identifies 11 strategic bets, 239 incremental extensions, 107 transformational long shots, 454 middle-range candidates, 41 low-priority products and 286 excluded products. The contribution is an auditable workflow that translates complexity metrics into a cautious policy-screening framework. The analysis is descriptive and does not claim causal identification.
