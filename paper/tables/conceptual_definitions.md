# Conceptual Definitions

| Concept | Working definition | Role in paper | Caveat |
|---|---|---|---|
| Economic growth | Change in aggregate income or output over time. | Used in the econometric section through GDP per capita growth outcomes. | Not equivalent to development or structural transformation. |
| Economic development | Broader improvement in living standards, productive capacity, and institutional capabilities. | Frames the policy relevance of diversification. | Not directly measured by trade data alone. |
| Structural transformation | Reallocation of production and capabilities toward more diverse and higher-productivity activities. | Central phenomenon motivating the paper. | Observed indirectly through export structure. |
| Diversification | Expansion of the export basket across products or sectors. | Measured through RCA diversity and product-level opportunities. | More products do not automatically mean higher sophistication. |
| Sophistication | Relative knowledge or capability intensity of products. | Approximated through projected PCI. | PCI is internally projected, not an external product-side eigenvector. |
| Economic complexity | Relative position of a country in the annual capability distribution inferred from the RCA matrix. | Measured through annually standardized ECI. | Longitudinal changes are relative, not absolute capability units. |
| Productive capabilities | Tacit and explicit know-how, institutions, inputs, and coordination that allow production. | Conceptual mechanism behind complexity and proximity. | Not directly observed in the data. |
| Revealed comparative advantage | Country-product export share relative to the world product share. | Defines MCP and current specialization. | RCA is a revealed export measure, not a full productivity measure. |
| Path dependence | The tendency for new activities to be related to existing capabilities. | Links Product Space proximity to feasible diversification. | Does not make upgrading impossible. |
| Proximity | Conditional co-export relatedness between two products. | Builds the Product Space. | Depends on observed export patterns and classification. |
| Density | Share of a product's capability neighborhood already present in Bolivia's RCA basket. | Measures feasibility relative to current capabilities. | High density is not a guarantee of profitability. |
| Opportunity Gain | Potential complexity benefit associated with moving toward a product. | Measures transformation potential in the opportunity screen. | Does not include implementation costs. |
| Feasibility Score | Relative score emphasizing density and near-term capability relatedness. | Organizes practical screening priorities. | Not an investment recommendation. |
| Transformation Score | Relative score emphasizing PCI and opportunity gain. | Identifies higher-upgrading candidates. | Requires stronger sector validation. |
